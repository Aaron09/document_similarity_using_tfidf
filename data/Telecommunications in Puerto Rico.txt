Telecommunications in Puerto Rico includes radio, television, fixed and mobile telephones, and the Internet.
Broadcasting in Puerto Rico is regulated by the US Federal Communications Commission (FCC).[1]

Radio[edit]
See also: List of radio stations in Puerto Rico

Stations: 140 radio stations (as of January 2015).[2][3]

Television[edit]
See also: List of television stations in Puerto Rico

Stations: more than 30 TV stations operating; three stations of the US Armed Forces Radio and Television Service; cable TV subscription services are available (2007).[4]
Television sets: 1.0 million sets (1997).[needs update]

Telephones[edit]
See also: Telephone numbers in Puerto Rico

Calling code-area codes: +1-787, +1-939[4]
International call prefix: 00[5]
Main lines: 780,200 lines in use, 87th in the world (2012).[4]
Mobile cellular: 3.1 million lines, 130th in the world (2012).[4]
System: modern digital system integrated with that of the United States (2011).[4]
Satellite earth stations: Intelsat with high-speed data capability (2011).[4]
Communications cables: provide connectivity to the US, Caribbean, Central and South America (2011).[4]

Internet[edit]

Top-level domain: .pr[4]
Internet users: 1.9 million users, 92nd in the world; 51.4% of the population, 83rd in the world (2012).[6][7]
Fixed broadband: available to 65% of Puerto Ricans (2012).[8]
Wireless broadband: available to 98.7% of Puerto Ricans (2012).[8]
Available broadband technologies as a percentage of population: 48.2% DSL, 0.1% Fiber, 59.6% cable, 84.7% wireless (2012).[8]
Internet hosts: 469 hosts, 184th in the world (2012).[4]
IPv4: 1 million addresses allocated, less than 0.05% of the world total, 250.2 addresses per 1000 people (2012).[9][10]
Internet Service Providers: 18 ISPs (1999).[needs update]

Internet censorship and surveillance[edit]
Further information: Internet censorship in the United States
Being a state dependency that controls the US territory, the country's Internet censorship is from the United States.
