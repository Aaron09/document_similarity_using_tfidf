Lophotrochozoa (/ləˌfɒtroʊkoʊˈzoʊə/, "crest/wheel animals") is a proposed clade of protostome animals. The taxon was established as a monophyletic group based on molecular evidence.[2][3]

Terminology[edit]
The word "lophotrochozoan" is sometimes equated with spiralian.[4] When used in a broader sense (sensu lato), it can include rotifers and platyhelminthes.[5] When used sensu stricto, it refers to a subgroup of Spiralia.
Groups[edit]
See also: List of bilaterial animal orders
The Lophotrochozoa has basal Cycliophora and Mollusca groups, and more derived Lophophorate, Nemertea and Annelida groups.[6][7]
